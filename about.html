About Us

Host Partners

The Conference will be hosted by Sunsound Tourism Consultancy in partnership with the Department of Tourism (DT), Lesotho Tourism Development Corporation (LTDC), Limkokwing University of Creative Technology (LUCT), and Lesotho Council for Tourism (LCT).

Lesotho Tourism Background

Over the years, Lesotho’s tourism has experienced continued growth and diversification making it one of the fastest growing sectors in the country.

The importance of tourism has been underscored in the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG’s 8, 12 and 14) of the United Nations 2030 Agenda which aims to acknowledge the importance of tourism as one of the key drivers of the world’s economic growth.

In Lesotho, tourism is recognized as a strategic tool for inclusive development and the sector has been integrated into the national and socio-economic agendas; National Strategic Development Plan (NSDP II).

The sector has enormous potential to create employment opportunities for both literate and illiterate people and spur economic development.

Conference BACKGROUND

Private Sector (BYTICA) in collaboration with DT, LTDC, LUCT and LCT will convene “The Lesotho Tourism Masutsa” within the broad strategic context of formulating a common approach and understanding on a range of key issues and challenges facing the tourism industry in Lesotho.

The Lesotho Tourism Masutsa Conference will be used as a platform where the private sector, the government and all other key stakeholders; i.e. developmental partners, educational institutions, financiers, etc. will be present to dialogue on issues relating to tourism development strategies and policies. In the same platform, business to business (B2B) networks will take place, networks will be created, business and products will be marketed, business deals will be signed, and corporates mandates will be discussed. The event will take place over a period of 2-3 days in order that all the planned objectives may be reached.

Purpose of the Conference

There are gaps identified in the industry that can be bridged by mutual understanding of all stakeholders, and this can be done through a Lesotho Tourism Masutsa Conference platform that will attract all tourism stakeholders and tourism value chains (from the districts of Lesotho) to meet tourism leaders, innovators and supporting organizations within the country.

The Lesotho Tourism Masutsa Conference is vital to Lesotho’s economy as it will encourage job creation, foster business networking and connections that are integral to the growth of the tourism sector.

The aim is to hold a 2-3 days event with a series of activities; wherein all tourism sector players will be invited in one room to discuss tourism matters that will shape the future of tourism in Lesotho.

Each sub sector will have representation and these will be selected from the Northern, Southern and Central regions of the country.

The ultimate goal is to have an annual event with the possibility of doubling the number of participants and inclusivity of youth aspiring to enter the tourism sector. The seminar will be hosted in September 2023 as a build up to World Tourism Celebration that’s celebrated globally.

The expected outcomes from this seminar are:

Establish local tourism business opportunities and market access

Gain insights into myriad of local tourism innovation opportunities

Learn how to build tourism supply chain linkages and opportunities with big industry stakeholders including leisure, MICE and travel technology;

Learn about post COVID-19 “new world” opportunities in terms of technology, financing and market access for start-ups, academia, investors, accelerators and venture builders;

Business networking for potential investorssourcing, public sector entities, start-ups, academia, corporations, accelerators and venture builders;

Meet local innovation and entrepreneurship ecosystem with tourism leaders supporting start-ups in scaling up their businesses;

Exchange of knowledge, business models and building partnerships

EVENT CONTENT

The focus areas for discussions at the event will among other be the following:

The importance of tourism attractions and infrastructures

Enhancing destination competitiveness in relation to marketing

Achieving transformation and skills- training in the sector

Tourism safety and security

Achieving better cooperation, not only between the private sector and Government but also among role players in similar environments.

Strengthened community involvement at all levels

Coordination and pooling of marketing, financial and product development resources

promoting ambassadorship amongst citizens of Lesotho
